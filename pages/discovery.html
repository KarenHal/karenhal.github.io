Schedule

DAY 1.

Khor virap. Etchmiadzin. Zvartnots
Your first day discovery will be the Monastery of
Khor Virap, which is the closest point to see Mount
Ararat. Next to Etchmiadzin - the spiritual center of
the Armenian Apostolic Church.
Armenia is the first country in the world that
adopted Christianity as a state religion (in 301 AD).
With more than five churches. Followed by Zvartnots
Cathedral, built in the 7th century.

DAY 2.

Kecharis. Hayravank. Noratus
Second day we will continue our journey to
Tsaghkadzor one of the most popular health resorts
in Armenia & also the ski resort town of Kecharis,
located in the North of the capital city Yerevan.
Followed by Lake Sevan, which is the largest, and
highest fresh water lake in the Caucasus region. The
Hayravank Monastery overlooks the lake, then to
Noratus where you will see more than 400 uniquely
carved khatchkars “cross stones” from as early 10th
century.

DAY 3.

Areni. Noravank. Wings Tatev
We head South of Yerevan towards Areni Wine
Factory. After some wine tastings continue the tour
to Noravank Monastery a masterpiece of Armenian
architecture. Followed by the village of Halidzor
where you'll hop on the longest cable car in the
world covering a distance of almost 6kms. While
enjoying breathtaking views of the Vorotan gorge
from above, you'll reach Tatev monastery complex,
which is one of the largest monasteries in Armenia.

DAY 4.

Saghmosavank. Ohanavank. Mughni
Saghmosavank monastery of the Psalms about 45
minutes North of Yerevan, the view of the gorge &
the Kasagh River is breathtaking. Mt Ara with its
mythical legends is also visible.
Ohanavank located south of Saghmosavank is rich
with inscriptions & carved scenes from the bible. It
stands majestically at the edge of the gorge
overlooking the Kasagh River. Come and admire the
spectacular scenery.
Saint Gevork 14th century monastery of Mughni,
Saint George "Slayer of Dragons". The interior has
beautiful frescoes. Return to Yerevan.
